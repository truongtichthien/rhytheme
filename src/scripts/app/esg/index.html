The Democratization

of IT

In years past, employees only used the most advanced technology at work - it was just too expensive to buy for consumer use. Now, technology makes it into the hands of employees quickly and relatively inexpensively, and IT organizations are struggling to keep up. Employees are more technologically savvy than ever before, bringing new devices and applications into the enterprise whether IT supports them or not.

Striking the Balance between IT Control and User Freedom

Mobility in the enterprise has the potential to transform how we work, accelerating productivity by taking friction out of collaboration, communication, and workflows. Employees are better aware of how these devices and applications can be used to drive the biggest productivity gains. If IT doesn't respond to employee demand for these applications and devices, employees are very willing to go around IT and use them anyway.

Given that employees are driving the demand for BYOD and enterprise file sync and share (EFSS) solutions, IT's biggest chance for success in keeping sensitive data out of rogue cloud services is to embrace employee input and make them a part of the policy and selection process.
